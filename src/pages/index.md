**Associate Professor · Department of Linguistics**
University of Example, Berlin

I work at the intersection of computational ABC, machine learning, and cognitive science. My research focuses on how large language models acquire and represent syntactic structure, and what this reveals about human language processing.

*Research areas: NLP · Syntax · Language Models · Cognitive Science*

## About

Before joining the University of Example, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics in Nijmegen (2018–2021) and completed my PhD at MIT in 2018 under the supervision of Prof. John Smith. My dissertation, *Hierarchical Structure in Neural Language Models*, received the ACL Dissertation Award in 2019.

I am broadly interested in questions about the nature of linguistic knowledge and how it emerges from exposure to language. My current work combines behavioral experiments, computational modeling, and formal syntactic theory.
